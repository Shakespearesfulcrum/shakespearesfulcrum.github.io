Actual Art

Artists subscribing to the Actual Art movement hold the conviction that art embraces the scientific reality of nature and the environment, its effects on physical materials, and factor in such forces as dictated by physics, chemistry, time, decay, and more, allowing their work to freely evolve with zero interference.  These artists recognize that some works may take tens, hundreds—if not thousands of years to reach ideal maturity.  They subscribe to the belief that no works are ever complete.  Rather than “finishing” works while also trying to incorporate methods to preserve them, this genre of artists embraces the evolution brought on by the environment and predict how they contribute to their works’ maturity over time. In fact, they often conceptualize their works based on such information, and create accordingly.   Actual Art works are recognized as just as important on their start dates as they are on any day in their near or distant future. They are equally significant from the day they are started as they are when the external forces take over and allow the works to evolve.  The physical process of change and the grand collaboration with nature/the environment is very much a part of the work, and greatly appreciated by such artists.
