Invisible Sculptures



In 1968, Tery Fugate-Wilcox set out to create sculptures that had perceivable depth and form, but were essentially invisible. Using multiple air pumps, small brass nozzles, valves, and vinyl tubes, air flow is adjusted with the help of a computer. When you move your hand across the space above the base, the changes in pressure create a tactile sensation allowing for the perception of a 3-dimensional object. The sculptures, with varying air pressure across hundres of nozzles, allow for a dynamic experience where the shapes morph from one object to another.

Fugate-Wilcox is revisiting this project in 2022. With the assistance of software engineers, he plans to create multiple interactive sculptures and bring this art to the public.
